2. Introduction and Definitions

Errors, in programming terms, represent an unintended deviation from the expected behavior of a program, which can result in unexpected results, crashes, or incorrect analyses. In pandas, errors can stem from various sources, including syntax mistakes, incorrect function usage, and data inconsistencies.

3. Core Concepts and Principles

Mastering pandas' core data structures—Series and DataFrame—is essential for error prevention. Pandas follows a principle of "lazy computing," meaning operations are not executed until a result is needed, allowing for seamless chaining of multiple operations. While this can enhance productivity, it also introduces potential errors if not properly managed.
